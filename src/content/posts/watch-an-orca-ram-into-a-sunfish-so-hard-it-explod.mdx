## The Details

Some also seem to enjoy destroying the carcasses of their kills like piñatas. Scientists documented this brutal activity among orcas ( Orcinus orca ) in the Gulf of California in 2024 and 2025.

In both cases, one adult orca held a dead sharp-tail sunfish ( Masturus lanceolatus ) steady while another slammed into it with enough force to smash the sunfish into smithereens. For our We summarize the week's scientific breakthroughs every Thursday.

## Why This Matters

Killer whales might pulverize sunfish to break them into smaller bites for young orcas — or they might just find it entertaining, researchers report July 23 in Frontiers in Ethology . “I was absolutely gobsmacked” , says Marianne Nyegaard, a marine biologist at Auckland Museum in New Zealand who was not involved in the work. She’d seen a couple videos of orcas battering sunfish on socials before the new paper did a deep dive on this phenomenon.

This could have implications for future research in this area.
