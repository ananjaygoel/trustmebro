## What's Happening

Real talk: Led by a trio of players 22 and younger, San Antonio dominated its first elimination game of this postseason to force Game 7 vs.

OKC If any part of you was still wondering whether the San Antonio Spurs were truly ready to skip all the traditional steps and win a title in their first postseason with this core, you just got your answer. There's still a long way to go. (shocking, we know)

Facing elimination for the first time in these playoffs, and for much of the roster the first time in their careers, the Spurs put on an absolute clinic on Thursday in a 118-91 win over the Thunder to even what has become a classic Western Conference Finals at three games apiece.

## The Details

It can't get here soon enough. Until then, let's take stock of what we just saw from a team that began this series with the youngest starting lineup in conference finals history.

Victor Wembanyama , Stephon Castle and Dylan Harper combined for 63 points, 21 rebounds and 15 assists in putting together, as a trio, a basically immaculate game. Castle, in his second season, is 21.

## Why This Matters

These guys are supposed to be in the absolute infancy of their NBA development. At the start of this year, anyone would've called it a huge step for the Spurs to simply make the playoffs, and here they are, in a back-against-wall conference finals game, putting the screws to the defending champs like a bunch of 10-year vets. Of course we have to talk about Wembanyama first.

Players and coaches will be watching how this unfolds closely.

## Key Takeaways

- After laying an egg in Game 5, he did everything he had to do to lead his team on Thursday.
- And when those first few fell, it was on from there as he finished with 22 of his 28 points in the first half.
