## Alzheimer's Awareness Month: A Reality Check

Lowkey, Alzheimer's disease is a wild card.  It's like, one minute your grandma is slaying the kitchen, and the next, she's struggling to remember what's for dinner.  November is Alzheimer's Awareness Month, and we need to talk about it.

## The Stats: It's a Big Deal  Did you know that 1 in 9 people over 65 have Alzheimer's? That's like, a major chunk of our grandparents' squad. In the US alone, 6.2 million people are living with the disease. It's giving me all the feels, honestly.  #

## Craig Olive's Story: Main Character Energy  Meet Craig Olive, the Johnston County Registrar of Deeds. His blended family has been hit hard by Alzheimer's. He's lost parents, siblings, grandparents, aunts, and uncles to the disease. It's a real-life drama, and I'm not even mad about it. Craig's story is a reminder that Alzheimer's affects families, not just individuals.  #

## What You Can Do: Get Involved, Fam  So, what's the tea? How can you join the fight against Alzheimer's? First, educate yourself. Learn the signs, symptoms, and risk factors. Then, get involved in your community. Participate in fundraising events, support research, and advocate for those affected.  #

## It's Time to Slay the Stigma  Alzheimer's disease is not just a memory loss issue; it's a complex condition that affects the whole family. It's time to slay the stigma surrounding the disease. Let's raise awareness, funds, and support for those impacted. We got this, fam!  #

## Final Thoughts: It's Giving Me Hope  November is Alzheimer's Awareness Month, and I'm ending on a high note. There's hope for a cure, and it's within our reach. Let's come together, get involved, and make a difference. Alzheimer's may be a wild card, but we can't let it win. It's giving me all the feels, and I'm lowkey excited about the future.
