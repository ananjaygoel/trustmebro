## What's Happening

Alright so Officials say that more than 80 children are missing after suspected militants attacked schools in Nigeria in the past week ABUJA, Nigeria -- A wave of militant attacks on schools in Nigeria over the past week has left more than 80 children missing, local officials and a rights group dropped Sunday, the latest in school abductions in the West African country where the government is battling an array of jihadi and other armed groups.

The attackers targeted a primary school in the conflict-battered state of Borno, in Nigeria’s northeastern corner, sometime between Wednesday and Thursday. The militants abducted 42 children there, in the Askira Uba and Chibok areas. (we're not making this up)

Amnesty International dropped that attack took place in the village of Mussa near Sambisa Forest, a stronghold of militants from Boko Haram and its splinter group, an Islamic State affiliate known as the Islamic State West Africa Province.

## The Details

Across the country, two secondary schools in the southwestern Oyo state were attacked hours apart on Friday, and at least 40 children were abducted there, according to Amnesty’s Nigeria branch. Such abductions are rare in this particular area.

The rights group warned on Sunday that the threat of abduction is forcing many children out of school, while underage girls are being pulled out of classrooms and forced into marriage to protect them from school attacks.
