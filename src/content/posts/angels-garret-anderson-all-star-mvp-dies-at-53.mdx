Longtime Angels outfielder Garret Anderson dies at 53 ESPN News Services Multiple Authors Apr 17, 2026, 12:52 PM ET Email Print Garret Anderson , the multitalented outfielder who became the Los Angeles Angels ' career hits leader and led the team to its only World Series title in 2002, has died at the age of 53, the team just dropped Friday. Anderson, who entered the majors with the then-California Angels in 1994 after being drafted in the fourth round in 1990, played 15 of his 17 major league seasons with the franchise and is a member of the team's hall of fame.
