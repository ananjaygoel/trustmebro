## What's Happening

So basically Four years ago, a developer called Jean Nguyen shared a short video on Dwitter of a spinosaurus pulling off a sick combo with Cloud Strife's Buster Sword.

This, of course, blew up, but it wasn't actually a game, just some animation work from the dev. And then, somewhere along the line, Nguyen decided to make it into an actual game. (let that sink in)

It's now called Dinoblade, it's out today, and it is jank and over top in exactly the right ways, and genuinely a rip roaring time.

The whole concept is deliciously silly. Some kind of cataclysmic event took place, and now dinosaurs all fight each other using various bladed weapons.

## Why This Matters

You, still a spinosaurus, seemingly forge a pact with some kind of mysterious maybe evil but I'm not quite sure Other Dinosaur, and upon doing so the fighting begins. The best immediate comparison mechanically is Sekiro, as you can parry attacks, but there's a bit more emphasis on the action element. Dinoblade | Release Date Trailer Watch on YouTube As ridiculous as the entire thing is, the combat genuinely feels good.

Gaming fans have strong opinions about moves like this, and for good reason.

## Key Takeaways

- Parrying is responsive, you've got quick and heavy attacks that make for some swish combos, and enemy animations are surprisingly readable.
- There is that aforementioned jank though, which mostly comes from the environments.

## The Bottom Line

It's a little awkward, a touch clunky, but I forgive this given that you are a giant ass dinosaur, the feeling at least makes sense with that in mind. Though, sometimes the enemies were invisible and the environments wouldn't quite load properly, but I think it was just one person making this to the end, so I can extend some grace there as well.
