## What's Happening

Here's the thing: Learn how extra whey protein affected muscle strength, muscle mass, and a muscle-related hormone in healthy older adults over 24 weeks.

A scoop of whey protein can change what shows up in the blood. But in a new trial of healthy older adults, that did not mean stronger legs, more muscle, or better physical performance. (plot twist fr)

Published in The American Journal of Clinical Nutrition , researchers tested whether boosting protein intake with whey could help protect aging muscles, especially because older bodies often respond less efficiently to protein than younger ones.

## The Details

They also tested potassium bicarbonate, an alkaline supplement meant to counter the acid load that can build up from protein metabolism. After 24 weeks, neither supplement improved muscle power, physical performance, lean mass, or muscle mass compared with placebo.

That was true even though both whey protein and potassium bicarbonate raised levels of IGF-1, a hormone involved in growth and muscle metabolism. : More Than a Muscle Booster, Creatine Could Help Energize the Elderly Testing Whey Protein for Muscle Strength in Older Adults With age, protein may not send the same muscle-building signal it once did.

## Why This Matters

Even when older adults consume enough amino acids for repair and maintenance, their bodies may respond more weakly than those of younger adults, a problem often called anabolic resistance. That has led some to suggest that older adults may need more protein than the current recommended daily allowance of 0. 8 grams per kilogram of body weight per day.

This could have implications for future research in this area.

## Key Takeaways

- Whey protein is often used for this purpose because it is easy to consume and rich in amino acids involved in muscle protein synthesis.
- The trial tested whether increasing intake well beyond that baseline would make a difference.
- Participants in the whey groups raised their protein intake to 1.
- 5 grams per kilogram per day, nearly double the current recommendation.

## The Bottom Line

Potassium bicarbonate was included for a different reason. Protein metabolism can add to the body’s acid load, and aging kidneys may become less able to clear that acid.
