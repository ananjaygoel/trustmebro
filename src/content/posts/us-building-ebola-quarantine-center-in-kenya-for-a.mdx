## The Details

00 EDT Last modified on Wed 27 May 2026 14. 16 EDT Prefer the Guardian on Google The Trump administration is building a quarantine and treatment center in Kenya for Americans affected by the Ebola outbreak, instead of bringing them home.

“The facility is designed to provide access to high-quality care for Americans who would need to quickly get out of DRC and quarantine without the risks of a lengthy transport back to the US,” a White House official told the Guardian. WHO chief calls for DRC ceasefire to tackle Ebola outbreak The center will also treat Americans who contract Ebola , the official dropped – “including critical care needs, though each case will be evaluated for forward transport for more advanced care as appropriate to maximize patient outcomes”.

## Why This Matters

The official did not clarify whether that further transport would be to the US or to Europe, where other Americans have been taken for quarantine and treatment. The official also did not clarify if Americans were allowed to return to the US if they did not want to go to Kenya .

World leaders are expected to respond to this situation soon.
