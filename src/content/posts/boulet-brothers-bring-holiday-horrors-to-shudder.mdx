## What's Happening Drag superstars The Boulet Brothers are about to inject some serious spook into your festive season. Their new anthology series, "The Boulet Brothers' Holiday of Horrors," is set to premiere on Shudder on December 16. This ghoulish gift features four original short films, promising a twisted take on holiday entertainment. Renowned directors like David Dastmalchian, Akela Cooper, and Kate Siegel join The Boulet Brothers themselves behind the camera. ## Why This Matters For fans of horror and drag, this is a major event, blending two vibrant subcultures into a unique holiday offering. It challenges the traditional, saccharine view of the season with something genuinely dark and exciting. The inclusion of names like David Dastmalchian (known for roles in "Dune," "The Suicide Squad") and Akela Cooper (writer of "M3GAN," "Malignant") alongside Kate Siegel (a muse for Mike Flanagan, starring in "The Haunting of Hill House") signals high-quality horror. Their involvement elevates this anthology beyond niche appeal. - It solidifies The Boulet Brothers' growing empire in the horror-drag space, expanding from their popular "Dragula" series.
- Shudder continues to cement its reputation as a premier destination for original, boundary-pushing horror content.
- This project offers a much-needed alternative to classic holiday fare, appealing to audiences looking for something truly different. ## The Bottom Line So, if you're tired of re-watching "It's a Wonderful Life" for the hundredth time, The Boulet Brothers are ready to offer a delightfully disturbing alternative. Are you ready to trade tinsel for terror this December?
