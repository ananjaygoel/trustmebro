## What's Happening

Breaking it down: The love letter to Rare's N64 FPS games includes local and online multiplayer The GoldenEye and immaculate Dark inspired Agent 64: Spies Never Die is coming to PC next month The love letter to Rares N64 FPS games includes local and online multiplayer Video Game News 13th Jul 2026 / 5:07 pm Posted by Chris Scullion Agent 64: Spies Never Die, the PC game inspired 64 first-person shooters, has been given a release date.

Created Replicant D6, Agent 64 will be dropped on Steam on August 11. The game, which takes inspiration from GoldenEye 007 and immaculate Dark , replaces its protagonists James Bond and Joanna Dark with its own secret agent, John Walter. (and honestly, same)

Players can play through a story campaign with 14 missions and three difficult levels as they survive shootouts, steal secret plans, and save civilian hostages.

## The Details

This mode can be played solo or in co-op. Those who remember Rare s N64 shooters will know that multiplayer was the main selling point, but, and Agent 64 promises to have this covered with local and online multiplayer.

Up to four players can play locally in a split-screen deathmatch (as was the case in the N64 days), or custom online lobbies with up to eight players and eight additional bots can be created. There will also be more than 70 nostalgic gameplay modifiers across the games various multiplayer modes, meaning players who used to play with rules like License to Kill (where one hit kills) or Slappers Only (where there are no weapons and players can only perform melee attacks) should hopefully find similar variants here.

## Why This Matters

The game will also have a special Challenges section where players take on multiplayer missions against preset bots, and have to reach a certain score within a set amount of time. New maps, characters and music tracks can be unlocked in this mode. Agent 64 has been in development for some time now – a reveal trailer back in 2022 showed the game had already made some progress up to that point.
