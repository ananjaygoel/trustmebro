The Alice Oseman-created British series—a dutiful Netflix adaptation of her graphic novels—never shied away from being unabashedly mushy while tackling its protagonists serious coming-of-age challenges, like mental health problems, eating disorders, bullying, and homophobia.

## The Details

This tonal tightrope is exactly why, for three seasons , it was easy to digest and necessary to watch, especially because of its joyful portrayals of various queer romances. While sticking to the source material and the genres trusted tropes, Heartstopper emerged as a countermeasure to some of the darker , more toxic love stories on TV.

Thankfully, it remains true to those strengths for the cozy finale movie Heartstopper Forever. Directed by Still Alice s Wash Westmoreland, Heartstopper Forever is just as much of a progressive, confident, and cheesy narrative PSA for viewers—not just teens!
