© Valve Valve is committing to shipping the Steam Machine , its console-like PC, and the Steam Frame VR headset this summer. The company locked in this release window via a new post that also d new details on how Valve is updating its Verified program for these new devices. (it feels like chaos)

Today, we are expanding the Verified program to include Steam Machine and Steam Frame, both of which are shipping this summer, dropped Valve in a blog post on the official Steam site .

## The Details

The company behind games like Half-Life and Portal didnt provide any other details on specifically when the devices will ship or, more importantly, how much they will cost at launch. Price is going to be a important factor in the success of the Steam Machine (and the Steam Frame.

) After Valve just increased the price of its popular handheld PC, the Steam Deck, by over $200, a lot of people are nervous about just how much a Steam Machine will cost. Valve says the Steam Machine is six times as powerful as a Steam Deck, which sounds solid as it will mean more games can be played on it, but it also sounds like it is going to be vastly more expensive than the Steam Deck, which now costs nearly $800 for the base OLED model .

## Why This Matters

The rest of the blog post is dedicated to Valve sharing details on how its Verified program, which shows people if a game is playable on the Steam Deck via a green check mark, will work on Steam Machine and Steam Frame. No surprises here, as the program will work basically the same way. Games will be tested by Valve, and if they pass, theyll get a green check mark.

The gaming community has been watching developments like this closely.

## Key Takeaways

- Valve says that if a developers game wasnt running well on Steam Deck, it will be checked again to see if it runs better on Steam Machine.
- If so, it will get a Steam Machine Verified badge.
