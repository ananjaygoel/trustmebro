## The Details

Its Metacritic score is hmm, and most people I speak to have never heard of it. But I have profoundly fond memories of this silly little game, and its all thanks to a jumbled-up used video game bargain bin at a GameStop where I found it at random one day over a decade ago.

Man, I miss those big, disorganized bins of unbelievably cheap, usually mediocre, used video games. For no reason whatsoever, Ive been thinking a lot about physical media since yesterday.

## Why This Matters

Specifically, Ive been thinking about my own physical game collection, and how Ive slowly moved away from physical games to digital over the years. There are a lot of reasons for that, most of them related to sheer convenience, but it occurred to me that one factor which has contributed to the dwindling of my physical game collection is the decreasingly frequency with which I encounter such bins out in the wild. A few GameStops still have used video game racks, and local game stores are often far more fruitful.

The gaming community has been watching developments like this closely.
