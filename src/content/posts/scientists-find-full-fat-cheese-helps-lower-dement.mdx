## The Details

Full-fat cheeses have been blamed as contributors for everything from obesity to heart disease, but surprising new research suggests cheeses like Brie and gouda may lower your risk of dementia. The connection seems random—and it is on some level—but the experts say there may be something here.

For the study, which was published in the journal Neurology , researchers analyzed data from nearly 28,000 people in Sweden with an average age of 58 at the start of the study. The participants were followed for an average of 25 years.

## Why This Matters

## The Bottom Line

Once they adjusted for things like age, sex, education, and overall diet quality, the researchers found that people who ate more high-fat cheese had a 13% lower risk of developing dementia compared to people who ate less. The high-fat cheese group also had a 29% lower risk of developing vascular dementia, which is caused interrupted blood flow to the brain.
