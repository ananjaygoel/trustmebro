## The Details

But it’s cold enough that I need to wrap up warmly or the chill seeps into my bones. Which brings me to the Neanderthals, our long-extinct cousins, who we have tended to imagine living in frigid environments.

A lot of our imagery of the Neanderthals is decidedly Siberian: frozen tundra, driving winds, woolly mammoths plodding through the snow. They have often been described as cold-adapted hominins.

## The Bottom Line

It had before been reported that Neanderthal nasal cavities contained specialised bony structures that helped them to warm up the air they had just breathed in. But these were not present in this spectacular specimen, suggesting they weren’t a standard Neanderthal feature.
