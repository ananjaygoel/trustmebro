My diet mainly consisted of processed food and grab-and-go snacks , and though I did the occasional cardio workout or bootcamp-style fitness class, nothing was ever consistent or sustainable.

## The Details

I was constantly tired, had practically no muscle tone, and was struggling to lose postpartum weight. It wasn’t until 2024 when my 1-year-old daughter broke her leg that I realized something needed to change.

I barely had the strength to carry her with the added weight of the cast and had zero additional energy to play with my 3-year-old son and juggle my demanding job. I felt depleted, and at 37, I wasn’t living the way I wanted to.

## Why This Matters

I knew I needed the structure and accountability of a coach. My friend introduced me to KMAK Fitness , an online training program that specializes in body recomposition through muscle growth and fat loss, with an emphasis on strength training and macro-based nutrition. She had gone through her own transformation with the help of a KMAK coach, and I was inspired , so I signed up for a consultation and connected with my coach George Pearce .

## Key Takeaways

- I was ready to put in the work, so I committed to virtually training three days a week.
- At first, I exclusively trained from home using dumbbells and free weights.
- I had little prior experience with weightlifting, so I kicked off out using light weights to get comfortable with movement patterns.

## The Bottom Line

Each 30- to 45-minute session focused on full-body compound movements (exercises that target multiple muscle groups at once), and I learned the importance of proper form. For added movement throughout the day, I also aimed for 10,000 daily steps .
