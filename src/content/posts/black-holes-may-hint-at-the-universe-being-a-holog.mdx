## The Details

But it was billed as the future of entertainment, because the cabinet insisted you were looking at a hologram. Time Traveler used a curved mirror to fake three-dimensionality.

Walk around the cabinet, and the illusion fell apart. A real hologram, the kind Dennis Gabor invented in the late 1940s and at some point clutched a Nobel Prize for, does something stranger: it stores a fully three-dimensional scene on a flat two-dimensional surface.

## Why This Matters
