## Why This Matters

This marks the best time to view the king of planets, as it will appear its brightest in the sky. The wild sight comes a little less than annually—once every 13 months—so be sure to get your eyes on the gas giant in all its glory. Any of the planets that lie outside Earth’s orbit can be seen at opposition from our perspective, meaning they’ve formed a line with the sun and Earth, with our planet in the middle.

Scientists and researchers are watching this development closely.
