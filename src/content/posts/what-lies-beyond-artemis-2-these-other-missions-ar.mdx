## The Details

NASA After rounding the moon , viewing a solar eclipse and traveling farther than any human had before , the four astronauts of Artemis 2—the first manned lunar mission in more than five decades—will re-enter Earth’s atmosphere tomorrow. Beyond those milestones, the mission represents a step toward establishing a long-term human presence in space and sets up the moon as a lens for considering the future of exploration.

“The moon is definitely where it’s at,” says Robert Braun , an aerospace engineer and the head of the Space Exploration Sector at the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab. Much will happen there in the next five years, he adds, to prepare it as a base for larger ventures to come.
