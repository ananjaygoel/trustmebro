## The Details

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) dropped its official forecast for the 2026 Atlantic hurricane season on . And its predicting a below-average hurricane season, specifically, 8-14 total named storms (this includes both tropical storms and hurricanes), 3-6 hurricanes and 1-3 major hurricanes.

Hurricane season runs from June 1 through November 30. And major hurricanes are those that reach categories 3, 4 or 5.

## Why This Matters

The major reason for the lower-than-average prediction is the emerging El Niño. In fact, NOAA says, we might see super El Niño conditions in the coming year. As of May 2026, NOAA is saying: El Niño is likely to emerge soon (82% chance in May-July 2026) and continue through Northern Hemisphere winter 2026-27 (96% chance in December 2026-February 2027).

This could have implications for future research in this area.

## Key Takeaways

- El Niño conditions happen when warmer-than-average water pools in the eastern Pacific near equatorial South America.
- Normally, cold, nutrient-rich water rises from the deep ocean in this area.
- But El Niño blocks the upwelling of this cold water.
- So it disrupts marine life and local fisheries.

## The Bottom Line

El Niño often brings a downstream trough of flowing winds over the Caribbean Sea and western tropical Atlantic. Its this trough that brings the wind shear that can disrupt hurricane formation or growth.
