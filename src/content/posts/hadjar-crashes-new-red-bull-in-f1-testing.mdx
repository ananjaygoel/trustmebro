## What's Happening

Not gonna lie, Newly promoted Red Bull driver Isack Hadjar crashes his car in wet conditions on the second day of Formula 1 pre-season testing.

Hadjar crashes new Red Bull in F1 testing Image source, Image caption, Hadjar took an L control of his Red Bull in the final corner in wet conditions By Andrew Benson F1 Correspondent Published 4 minutes ago Red Bull driver Isack Hadjar crashed his car in wet conditions on the second day of Formula 1 pre-season testing in Barcelona. The 21-year-old Frenchman, to the senior Red Bull team for 2026 after just one season in F1, took an L control at the fast final corner and hit the barriers. (we're not making this up)

The test is being held in private with no access for independent media, and Red Bull have not responded to requests for comment on the incident.

## The Details

But eyewitnesses at the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya say Hadjar was on his first lap on a set of intermediate tyres having just switched from full wet tyres. The track was still damp and the weather was drizzly when he went off, spinning and hitting the barrier backwards.

Until then, Red Bull had continued their encouraging progress of the first day, and Max Verstappen had had his first run in the car in the morning session. The four-time champion had an off at Turn Five on his first lap out of the pits but was able to rejoin.
