## The Details

After all, rival boards, especially those based on the x86 architecture, have gotten majorly cheaper over the years, while the mainline RPi SBCs have crept up in pricing without offering tangible benefits in DIY projects. While I still have a collection of Raspberry Pi systems I’ve bought over the years, I’ve stopped buying them after the RAM shortage drove their prices to the red zone, and switched to old thin clients for server experiments.

On the smart home front, but, I’ve transitioned to microcontrollers. To be precise, I’ve kicked off using ESP32 boards for the majority of my smart home experiments.

## Why This Matters

Yeah, I’m aware that microcontrollers and SBCs are entirely different technologies, but the former are not only more cost-effective, but they’re easier to program and boast better compatibility with my sensor modules.
