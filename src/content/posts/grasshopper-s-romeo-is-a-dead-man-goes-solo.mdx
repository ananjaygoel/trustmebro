This time, the studio, led by the distinctive Suda51, is taking the independent route by self-publishing the game. Suda51 couldn't resist a playful jab at the competition, joking that Grasshopper has 'won its game of chicken' against the highly anticipated Grand Theft Auto 6. It's a bold, humorous statement from a studio known for its unique flair. ## Why This Matters Grasshopper's decision to self-publish 'Romeo is a Dead Man' is a significant strategic move. This grants Suda51 and his team complete creative and financial autonomy, allowing them to fully realize their artistic vision without external publisher constraints. However, it also means shouldering the entire marketing and distribution burden. Suda51's humorous jab at Grand Theft Auto 6 isn't just a funny quip; it's a savvy marketing play.
