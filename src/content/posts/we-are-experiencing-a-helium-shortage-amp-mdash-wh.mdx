## What's Happening

Here's the thing: Used in everything from critical medical technology to AI development, helium is irreplaceable.

Learn how Earth is running out of it. Helium is one of those chemical elements we’ve all heard of, but most of us only know about it for its most trivial uses. (wild, right?)

It’s solid for birthday balloons, keeps blimps and Thanksgiving Day parade inflatables aloft, and has allowed countless jokers a chance to amuse their friends with comically high-pitched voices.

It’s also in short supply on Earth, which is a problem in many ways. Based on current economics and available technology, a mere fraction of Earth’s helium — roughly 40 billion cubic meters — is available for practical extraction and use.

## Why This Matters

That may sound like a lot, but many estimates suggest that unless we find new sources or develop some as-yet-unknown technology to efficiently extract the gas from the Earth, our available helium supply could be exhausted in as little as a century. If that happens, many areas of scientific research will be devastated, or at least made prohibitively expensive. Another issue with helium’s scarcity is that it lacks a practical substitute on the periodic table.

Scientists and researchers are watching this development closely.
