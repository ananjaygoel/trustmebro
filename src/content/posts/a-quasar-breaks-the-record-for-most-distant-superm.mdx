## The Details

A newly spotted quasar — a black hole that is gobbling matter so quickly it glows white-hot — sends light from just 662 million years after the Big Bang, astronomers report July 6 in Astronomy & Astrophysics . The previous record holder , found out in 2021, is from 15 million years later.

The discovery ratchets up the tension on a longstanding mystery of how early black holes got so big so fast , says astronomer Daming Yang of Leiden University in the Netherlands. “Every step further back in time is making this question even harder to explain.
