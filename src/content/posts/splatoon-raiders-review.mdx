One of the micro-trends I've been happiest to spot in the last few years is Nintendo's embracing of what you could term apocalyptic simplicity .

## The Details

And by micro-trends, I mean this: I've spotted it in just two games. Splatoon Raiders review Developer: Nintendo Publisher: Nintendo Platform: Played on Switch 2 Availability: Out 23rd July on Switch 2 The first of these was Donkey Kong Bananza , a wonderfully propulsive beat-'em-up in which the 'em you were often beating up was the environment itself.

Bananza was all about being powerful and fast and using your fists to smash through the earth, turning each carefully crafted level into so much gravel and sand. It was all about digging through stuff until there was no stuff left.

## Why This Matters

And it turned out to be Nintendo's most joyous game in quite a while. And now Splatoon Raiders is here. It's a third-person shooter, but it piles on so many enemies, often of such solid size, that you're frequently blasting away at anything and everything around you.

The gaming community has been watching developments like this closely.

## Key Takeaways

- You drop into a level, you kill pretty much everything that spawns, and then you collect any trinkets that are left behind afterwards.
- Reader, it is bloodthirsty stuff.
- And like Bananza, it's a game that would rather fling more at you than you can parse than slow down for a second.
- To put it another way, both games pursue absolute carnage so thoroughly that the camera sometimes can't fr keep up.
