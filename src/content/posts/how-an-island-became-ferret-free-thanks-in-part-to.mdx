## The Details

More than 400 traps were laid across the island as part of a five-year, £4. 5m Life Raft project, which has also targeted rats.

But crucial to the fight to save the seabirds was a specially-trained detection dog, called Woody. The island is now ferret free and there are already encouraging signs in breeding seabird numbers, offering hope to other places round the world grappling with invasive predators.
