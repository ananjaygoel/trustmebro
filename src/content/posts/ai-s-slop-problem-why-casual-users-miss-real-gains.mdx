## What's Happening Three years ago, ChatGPT launched, sparking unprecedented investment and excitement in AI. Today, that enthusiasm has soured, with public sentiment turning sharply negative. This shift accelerated with OpenAI's GPT-5 release this summer, which received mixed reviews from casual users. They judged the system by its surface flaws, ignoring its true underlying capabilities. ## Why This Matters Dismissing AI based on superficial 'slop' isn't just a casual user's mistake; it's becoming a serious enterprise risk. Companies risk falling behind by misjudging AI's rapid, if often subtle, advancements. While casual users focus on minor errors, AI's core capabilities are improving significantly. This widening gap between perception and reality has critical implications for businesses. - Businesses making poor investment decisions based on flawed public perception.
- Enterprises missing critical competitive advantages as rivals adopt advanced AI.
- Organizations underestimating AI's potential for future market disruption.  ## The Bottom Line The narrative around AI is currently dominated by negativity and surface-level criticism.  But for those paying close attention to underlying capabilities, the AI 'toddler' is growing up fast.  Are businesses ready to look past the 'slop' and truly understand what's coming next?
