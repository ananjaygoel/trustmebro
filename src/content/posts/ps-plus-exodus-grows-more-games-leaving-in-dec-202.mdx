## What's Happening

Sony has once again expanded the list of games set to depart from PS Plus Extra and Premium, specifically for December 2025.  This marks the second time the company has quietly added titles to the removal list since its initial announcement.

Just two weeks ago, Sony provided the official confirmation of December's departures.  However, the PS Store's 'Last Chance to Play' section has since been updated twice, adding more games to the chopping block without fanfare.

These recent updates have collectively brought the total number of *additional* games leaving the service to three.  This means subscribers are seeing more titles disappear than initially communicated, and for a date surprisingly far in the future.

## Why This Matters

For PS Plus Extra and Premium subscribers, this rolling list of departures is a significant headache.  Gamers rely on the announced library to plan their play sessions, and last-minute changes disrupt that experience and their gaming queue.

The quiet, incremental additions to the departure list erode trust between Sony and its player base.  When initial announcements aren't final, it casts doubt on the transparency and reliability of the service's offerings.

It also impacts the perceived value of the subscription.  If games can be pulled without clear, definitive notice, the long-term appeal of building a backlog from the PS Plus library diminishes.

Players might feel pressured to rush through titles they otherwise wouldn't, fearing their imminent removal.  The specific date, December 2025, adds another layer of confusion.  Why are 'Last Chance to Play' warnings appearing *now* for games leaving over a year from now?

This unusually long lead time makes the quiet, repeated updates even more perplexing for members.  This pattern could stem from various issues, such as ongoing licensing negotiations or internal communication breakdowns that lead to fragmented information releases.

Regardless of the underlying cause, the effect is a less predictable and potentially frustrating experience for paying customers.  Ultimately, players expect consistency and respect for their time and money.

When the terms of their subscription feel like a moving target, it undermines the overall appeal and perceived quality of the PS Plus service.

## The Bottom Line

Sony's repeated, quiet additions to the PS Plus departure list, especially for games leaving in December 2025, are creating unnecessary confusion and frustration.  Subscribers expect a definitive roster, not a constantly evolving one.

Transparency and consistent communication are absolutely key to maintaining subscriber loyalty in a competitive market.  This current approach risks alienating the very players who invest their hard-earned money in Sony's premium services.

Will Sony learn from this feedback and commit to more stable, upfront announcements regarding game departures in the future?  Or will PS Plus members have to continually monitor the 'Last Chance to Play' section for unexpected removals, forever guessing what's next?
