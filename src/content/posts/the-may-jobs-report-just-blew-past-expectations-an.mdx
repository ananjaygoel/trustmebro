## What's Happening

So get this: The labor market delivered 172,000 jobs in May, past the expectation for 88,000.

The labor market delivered another whopping win on Friday: 172,000 jobs in May, when economists had expected 88,000. Recommended Video The even stronger surprise was in the rearview mirror: March was revised up by 29,000 to 214,000, April by 64,000 to 179,000. (and honestly, same)

Combined, the two months were 93,000 stronger than before reported.

## The Details

Though such a strong labor market is good news for those fearing the economy was stalling—or that AI was eating jobs—it could make investors wince. The strong print could kill what has been left of rate-cut hopes for this year, especially as inflation persists and the conflict in Iran drags on.

The 10-year bond yield jumped about 6 basis points to above 4. 3%, but, where it’s been parked since last July, so the report’s other wildcard is defused.

## Why This Matters

The job market is rebounding in 2026 after a limpish 2025. After all the revisions were dropped and done, the economy added roughly 10,000 jobs a month in 2025. So far in 2026, it’s averaging 114,000.

Market watchers are paying close attention to developments like this.

## Key Takeaways

- Under the hood, but, the hiring is still relatively concentrated.
- Leisure and hospitality added 70,000 jobs in May—five times its 12-month average—with restaurants and bars alone hiring 48,000.
- Local government added 55,000, and health care 35,000.

## The Bottom Line

For the Fed, which meets June 16–17, a labor market this resilient is yet one more reason to sit on its hands. 75% mean borrowing costs—particularly mortgage rates—stay elevated too.
