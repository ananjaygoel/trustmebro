British Gas has agreed to pay £20m into a redress fund and compensate users to settle an investigation regulator into the forced fitting of prepayment meters. (yes, really)

Ofgem found the company had "flopped to meet the standards required" when installing the meters and had breached licence conditions specifically aimed at protecting users in vulnerable situations.

## The Details

Three years ago it emerged that debt agents working for British Gas had broken into the homes of vulnerable users to fit prepayment meters. British Gas has apologised, and the complete settlement package will cost the company up to £112m in payments, compensation and writing off users debt.

In 2023, the Times spilled how agents working for Arvato Financial Solutions, on behalf of British Gas, had forced their way into the home of a single father of three to install a prepayment meter. After establishing the property was unoccupied, an undercover reporter observed the agents work with a locksmith to force their way in and install a meter.

## Why This Matters

The scandal was industry-wide: 40,000 users had a prepayment meter installed without permission between 2022 and 2023. On and Scottish Power have already agreed to pay compensation. Ofgem investigated and subsequently banned the practice of fitting prepayment meters without users permission in high-risk households.
