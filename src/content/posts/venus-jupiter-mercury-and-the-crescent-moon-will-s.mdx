## What's Happening

Breaking it down: Learn how to see Venus, Jupiter, Mercury, the crescent Moon, and the Gemini stars Pollux and Castor in June 2026's evening sky.

Step outside after sunset this month and look west. Two of the brightest objects in the night sky are sitting close together, and they’re hard to miss. (shocking, we know)

Venus and Jupiter are both visible in the western sky throughout June 2026, with Mercury joining them later in the month.

## The Details

Around June 16 and 17, 2026, the crescent Moon and the Gemini stars Pollux and Castor move into the same part of the sky, giving skywatchers one of June’s best evening views. When to See Venus, Jupiter, and Mercury Venus is the brighter of the two and easy to find, a steady white light that outshines everything around it.

Jupiter sits nearby , and the two reach their closest point around June 8 and 9, 2026, when they appear near enough together to look almost like a double star, according to NASA. Look west as the sky darkens, roughly an hour after sunset.

## Why This Matters

From June 11 through 15, Mercury drops into view below Venus and Jupiter in the western twilight, according to the Planetary Society. It reaches its highest point above the horizon on June 15. Mercury never strays far from the Sun from our point of view on Earth, so it tends to linger close to the horizon and can fade into the twilight glow.

- When their orbits line up from our perspective, they cluster together in a way that can look almost choreographed.
- The four objects stretch in a loose line toward the horizon.
