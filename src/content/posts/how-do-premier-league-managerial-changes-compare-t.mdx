What is notable, but, is the condensed number of clubs involved, and the lack of time given to those managers who didnt leave of their own accord.

## The Details

In 2022-23, 11 different teams – more than half of the top flight – changed their manager; this season there have been just six clubs involved, with half of those doing so more than once. Tottenham and Chelsea have both changed managers twice, while Nottingham Forest are on their fourth of the season.

Its just the second time – after 2022-23 – that three different Premier League clubs have made at least two managerial changes in a season. In 2022-23, the managers leaving their jobs had been in position for almost twice as long as those who have left this season.

## Why This Matters

While not all departures that season were a one-way thing – for example, Graham Potter chose to leave Brighton for Chelsea – its generally still noticeable how much more time managers were given before clubs took action. Javier Gracias reign at Leeds was the most short-lived in 2022-23. Brought in with the task of saving the Whites from relegation, he was sacked after 10 weeks and Sam Allardyce, who had history of saving clubs from relegation brought in for the final four games.
