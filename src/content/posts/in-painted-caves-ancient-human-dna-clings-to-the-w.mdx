## The Details

The search took place at caves throughout Spain and Portugal known for prehistoric paintings, including Spains famed Cave of Altamira. The DNA recovered from cave paintings and near at least 2,000 years old and probably much older, researchers report June 23 in Nature Communications .

The study cannot link the DNA to the artists behind the cave paintings. But the knowledge that human DNA can be preserved on cave walls for thousands of years may enable future work that probes the role of women in ancient art, traces the movement of artists and brings clarity to the debate over Neandertals artistic abilities , says Hipólito Collado Giraldo, an archaeologist with Spains regional government of Extremadura.
