## What's Happening

Listen up: The Lampard Inquiry hears from the experts about Oxevision technology.

Evidence lacking for mental health monitoring tech Image source, Richard Daniel/BBC Image caption, An inquiry into the deaths of 2,000 people in Essex is expected to continue into 2027 By Nikki Fox , East of England health correspondent and Louise Parry Published 3 hours ago There is no "strong independent evidence" that a monitoring system used widely in mental health wards has improved patient safety, an inquiry has heard. The Lampard Inquiry is examining the deaths of more than 2,000 mental health patients in Essex over a 24-year period. (shocking, we know)

The inquiry had before heard "grave concerns" expressed about the widespread use of Oxevision technology , which monitors patients in their rooms using infrared sensors and cameras.

## The Details

LIO Health, which runs Oxevision, just dropped: "Technology has a vital role to play in supporting safer inpatient mental health care, an area without any medical device technology until Oxevision. " It added its impact had been "significant".

"In one trust alone, the technology has helped staff in responding to 1,453 safety incidents over a four-year period," LIO Health dropped. On Thursday, the inquiry heard from two the experts from the University of Oxford.

## Why This Matters

They had reviewed 45 studies that were used to justify introducing Oxevision across the NHS. The academics found some of the studies involved employees of Oxevision or were not extensive enough. They concluded the studies did "not provide strong independent evidence of improved patient safety, acceptability or cost effectiveness".
