## The Details

The Artemis 2 spacecraft and its four astronauts are finally heading home, following a historic day in human spaceflight yesterday. At around 18 UTC on , the crew reached the farthest distance from Earth ever traveled by humans, breaking the record of 248,655 miles (400,171km) set 13 crew in 1970.

And a few hours later, Artemis 2 began its voyage around the moons far side. As they soared some 4,600 miles (7,400 km) above the lunar surface, the brave astronauts had no contact with Earth for 40 minutes.

## Why This Matters

This communications outage was expected, as they moon was blocking all signals between our planet and the spacecraft. It was during that time that the crew set the new distance record: 252,756 miles (406,771km), the greatest distance humans have ever traveled from Earth. They also became the first humans to see parts of the moons far side in daylight.
