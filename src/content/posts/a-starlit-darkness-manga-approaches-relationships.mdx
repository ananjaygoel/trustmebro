## What's Happening

Real talk: A Starlit Darkness is the new manga from the Cherry Magic creator, and it depicts a different relationship dynamic.

Square Enix picked up Toyota’s newest series for a worldwide release, and it is quite different for tone and genre.

## The Details

While the manga A Starlit Darkness also focuses on what looks like it will become an intense relationship between two leads, it’s darker and more thrilling than Toyota’s last romanic comedy. A Starlit Darkness is set in Taisho era Japan and begins with a struggling, deeply in debt writer named Subaru Karasuma heading to his neighbors to get back the coal brazier he lent them. He learns the five are also a group of writers collaborating on a self-published magazine alongside well-known and successful author Geppo Yagira.

## Why This Matters

Geppo ASAP clocks that Subaru is considering using the burner to commit suicide poisoning and, instead, offers to pay him to contribute to the group’s next issue to replace someone who dropped out and give him some money. In so doing, he basically goads and bullies him into contributing. What follows is fascinating, as we’re seeing how Subaru is dealing with his depression while interacting with a man who is essentially exactly the kind of person he wanted to be.

Gaming fans have strong opinions about moves like this, and for good reason.
