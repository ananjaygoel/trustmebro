## The Details

The organelle doesn’t belong to cows. It’s part of fuzzy single-celled protozoa called ciliates.

The microbes live in cattle’s rumens, the first stomach of cud-chewing animals where grass and other plants are fermented and broken down. The new organelle is called a hydrogenobody .

## Why This Matters

It makes hydrogen, which then stimulates other microbes in the rumen to produce the greenhouse gas methane, researchers report April 30 in Science .
