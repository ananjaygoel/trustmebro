You’ve had a full night of sleep, you’re not wildly overworked, and you eat reasonably well. And yet, by mid-afternoon, you feel like you’re dragging yourself through wet concrete. (yes, really)

A new study published in Nutrients found the culprit might be hiding in your bloodstream.

## The Details

Researchers analyzed blood samples and fatigue questionnaires from about 600 healthy adults in Japan and found that people with higher levels of a molecule called homocysteine were more likely to report physical exhaustion if they were men, or notably lower motivation, if they were women. “Blood homocysteine levels have traditionally raised concerns in relation to cardiovascular disease, dementia, and fractures.

But, our findings suggest that attention should also be paid to fatigue and motivation in the future. To prevent an increase in homocysteine levels, it is important to avoid deficiencies in vitamin B12 and folate.

## Why This Matters

Maintaining a well-balanced diet on a daily basis is essential,” dropped lead author Hiroaki Kanouchi in a press release . How Vitamin B12 and Folate May Be Linked to Fatigue Homocysteine is produced naturally during normal metabolism. The body relies on folate and vitamin B12 to help process it, so when those vitamins run low, homocysteine can rise.

This could have implications for future research in this area.

## Key Takeaways

- People with higher homocysteine consistently had lower levels of both folate and vitamin B12.
- The relationship held even after accounting for sleep, working hours, exercise habits, age, body mass index, kidney function, and diet.
