## What's Happening Today marked the end of an era for F1 fans in the US, as ESPN broadcast its final race for the 2025 championship. Starting next year, Apple TV is officially taking the wheel for all Formula 1 streaming. This means every race will be available exclusively as part of an Apple TV subscription. The tech giant even dropped a new social media ad today, giving us a sneak peek at the F1 channel's sleek look within the TV app and confirming driver onboard cameras will be part of the package. ## Why This Matters This shift is a huge deal for F1 enthusiasts and the broader streaming landscape, marking a significant change in how fans will consume the sport. The move to Apple TV consolidates all races under one subscription, simplifying access for many. For Apple, this is a massive play in the live sports arena, significantly bolstering their streaming service's appeal and subscriber base against competitors. ESPN, on the other hand, says goodbye to a major sports property. Here's why this matters most:
- **Exclusive Access:** All F1 races will now be housed exclusively within an Apple TV subscription, streamlining how fans catch every Grand Prix.
- **Immersive Viewing:** The confirmed driver onboard cameras promise an unprecedented, up-close perspective of the race, adding a new layer of excitement and strategic insight.
- **Streaming Market Shake-up:** Apple's aggressive move firmly plants them deeper into the competitive live sports streaming market, challenging traditional broadcasters and other tech giants.  ## The Bottom Line With Apple TV now geared up to deliver all F1 races, complete with immersive driver views, the future of motorsports streaming looks both consolidated and exciting.  It's a bold move that could redefine how we experience live sports.  Are you ready to trade your ESPN login for an Apple TV subscription?
