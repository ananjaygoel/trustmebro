So, you know how Google's always trying to be the main character in the tech industry?

Well, they really said 'hold my coffee' and announced they're in talks to sell their custom AI chips to other companies.

## The Tea ☕

These chips, called tensor processing units (TPUs), are like the secret sauce that makes Google's AI so good.  And now, they're looking to share that sauce with others, which is kinda a big deal.

I mean, imagine having a chip that's specifically designed for AI - it's like having a superpower, fr fr.

And the best part?  It's giving Nvidia, the current chip king, some serious competition.

## Why This Matters (Or Doesn't) 👀

This is lowkey a whole thing and I'm not okay, because it could change the game for AI development.

The people who actually know things are saying that Google's TPU sales could lead to more companies creating their own AI-powered products, which is pretty based.

But, on the other hand, it's also a bit sus that Google's trying to sell their tech to others.  Like, what's the real motive here?

Are they trying to make a quick buck, or do they really want to 'democratize' AI (yes, really)?
