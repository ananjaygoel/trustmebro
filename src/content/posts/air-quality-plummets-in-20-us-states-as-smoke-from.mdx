## The Details

21 EDT First Thu 16 Jul 2026 10. 49 EDT Prefer the Guardian on Google Smoke from wildfires burning in south-central Canada and parts of Minnesota is spreading across the US, prompting air quality alerts in more than 20 states with millions of Americans expected to face unhealthy air conditions this week.

The smoke from the more than 180 active wildfires in northern Ontario made Chicago’s air quality the worst in the world on Thursday evening, followed Minneapolis, according to IQAir’s global rankings. The smoke has spread across several US states, from Minnesota, where multiple wildfires are also burning, to New York state, blanketing the skies in haze and worsening air quality.
