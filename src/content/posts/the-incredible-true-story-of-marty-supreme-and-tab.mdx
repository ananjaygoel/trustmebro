## Why This Matters

Not unlike Chalamet’s charming grifter, Reisman was a Jewish table tennis champion who grew up in New York City and began seeking stardom in the rising sport (yes, it counts ) of ping-pong in the 1940s. Thanks to his slim build and eccentric self-presentation, he became known as Marty “The Needle” Reisman. Marty Supreme ’s screenplay, cowritten his longtime collaborator Ronald Bronstein, even loosely adapts The Money Player , Reisman’s 1974 autobiography that’s almost hilariously hard to track down now .
