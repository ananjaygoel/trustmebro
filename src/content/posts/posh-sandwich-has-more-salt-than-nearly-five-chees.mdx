## The Details

Action on Salt & Sugar, which analysed 546 sandwiches, dropped it was "alarming" how much salt was in them and that people should not be exposed to a "hidden health risk every time they buy lunch". Gails has been approached for comment.

Too much salt leads to high blood pressure, which is known as "the silent killer" as it raises the risk of heart attacks and stroke. Action on Salt & Sugar, based at Queen Mary University of London, found more than one in 10 sandwiches exceeded government salt targets and 44% would have to carry a red warning label on the packet because of their salt content.
