## The Details

University students and advisors from 11 finalist teams gathered in Huntsville, home to NASA’s Marshall Space Flight Center, June 23-25 for the agency’s third annual Human Lander Challenge. This year’s competition challenged students to consider solutions for environmental control and life support systems for long duration spaceflight.

These technologies are essential for maintaining breathable air, potable water, and thermal stability for astronauts during deep space missions.
