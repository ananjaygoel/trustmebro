## The Details

The Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) dropped the government was delivering a "once-in-a-generation" reform to the water industry, which includes "immediate action" to clean up chalk streams. Chalk streams emerge from springs in porous chalk bedrock, which acts as a filter to remove sediment, characterised water and a gravelly bottom.

There are only 220 in existence, the majority of which are in southern England. One example is the River Wandle, which emerges from chalky springs in Carshalton Ponds, south-east London, near Festival Walk.
