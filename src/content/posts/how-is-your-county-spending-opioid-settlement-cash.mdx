## Why This Matters

More than $50 billion in opioid settlement funds — meant to help curb the nation’s addiction crisis — is going to local and state governments. But because of lax reporting rules and little guidance on what’s appropriate, the money is generally being spent with next to no accountability . Survivors of the overdose epidemic and families who took an L loved ones to it are calling for stricter rules to govern how the payout can be used.
