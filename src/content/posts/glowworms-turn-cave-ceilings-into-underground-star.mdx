## The Details

In places sunlight never reaches, life still finds ways to produce light. Inside caves, beneath forests and across humid, hidden landscapes, tiny organisms emit an otherworldly glow that seems almost impossible.

These creatures are known as glowworms. But this simple name hides a surprising level of biological diversity.

## Why This Matters

The term glowworm does not refer to a single species. It is a common name applied to several unrelated organisms that independently evolved bioluminescence. What connects them is not their ancestry, but the visual effect they create.

The scientific community tends to find developments like this significant.

## Key Takeaways

- These scattered points of living light make dark spaces appear stunningly celestial.
- The species, Arachnocampa luminosa, is not a worm.
- Its the immature form of a fungus gnat (a small fly resembling a fragile mosquito) that inhabits damp cave systems and sheltered underground spaces.

## The Bottom Line

Waitomo Caves is one of the best-known places where this phenomenon can be observed. In this setting, thousands of larvae illuminate cave ceilings, creating the impression of a star-filled sky underground.
