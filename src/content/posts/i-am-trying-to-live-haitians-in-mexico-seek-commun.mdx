In his native Haiti , Gensley, 37, worked as a radio journalist and social worker, analyzing the effects of gang violence in some of Port-au-Prince ’s most dangerous neighborhoods.

With time, as his research led to police intervention, he caught the attention of the city’s gangs. In November 2024, during a period of escalating violence in the Haitian capital, gang members entered the compound where Gensley lived.
