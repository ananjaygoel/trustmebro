© Nic Taylor Steam might be a solid storefront for saving money when big sales roll around, but based on the fact that many users have heaps of games that languish unplayed in their libraries for ages, it’s also a solid storefront for wasting money. A solo developer has decided to transform this fact into the most meta game about games I think I’ve ever seen, as Nic Taylor’s upcoming indie game Game Quest: The Backlog Battler will allow you to fight your unplayed games in a gladiatorial arena. (let that sink in)

This one has taken the the internet , as several posts have kicked off cropping up on X over the past 48 hours about Taylor’s project.

## The Details

The concept of the game is simple; the more games you haven’t played, the more enemies you’ll face. Game Quest: The Backlog Battler doesn’t just allow you to fight your own Steam backlog, but, as there’s also an option to fight your Steam friends libraries of unplayed games (provided they’ve made their shameful backlogs public).

## Why This Matters

For instance, while your least-played Steam games will accost you during combat, your most-played games will cheer for you in the stands and, occasionally, even appear during the fight to aid you. I admittedly did get a chuckle out of seeing Baldur’s Gate 3 magically appear out of thin air to help aid me in my fight while I was getting my ass beat by Baldurs Gate II: Enhanced Edition .
