Produced in August 2025, the image has been nicknamed “the hummingbird” . The colors show differences in the way polarized microwave signals, which vibrate in different directions, interact with and reflect from the ice.

## Why This Matters

Over Antarctica, NISAR transmits radar waves toward Earth with a horizontal polarization. The orientation of the signals that return, either horizontal, vertical, or both, provide clues about the object or surface that reflected them. Signals that come back with a horizontal polarization likely bounced off a more regular surface, such as smooth ice.
