## What's Happening

Here's the thing: Rings can interpret gestures better than cameras or gloves, according to the study.

These rings can quickly turn sign language into text. Researchers in South Korea developed a wearable system that uses seven smart rings to read finger and hand motions to translate American Sign Language and International Sign Language into text. (it feels like chaos)

The purpose is to make communicating easier between those who sign and nonsigners without needing a separate human interpreter.

## The Details

 According to the study , published Friday in the journal Science Advances, the system reliably recognized 100 ASL and ISL words during testing. It also performed well with users the system had not seen before, and it didn t require recalibration for each person.

Because the system detects words in sequence, it can produce sentence-level translations without extra training on grammar. ASL and ISL are the everyday languages of more than 72 million deaf and hard-of-hearing people.

## Why This Matters

But, most hearing people do not know any words in these languages or have a basic understanding. That gap makes certain tasks, like ordering at a restaurant or asking for help, much more difficult. A concept of how the rings work in the real world.

## The Bottom Line

 To solve these problems, the researchers designed sensing rings for each finger that can capture precise motion and finger position while letting the hands move naturally. The rings can detect both signs that involve movement, like the words for "dance," "fly" and "sun," and signs that are held still, like "I" and "you.
