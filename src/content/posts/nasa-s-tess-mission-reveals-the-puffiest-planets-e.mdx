## The Details

NASA / Daniel Rutter Data from NASA’s TESS (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) mission has spilled two new “super-puff” planets, giant worlds so light that their density is comparable to cotton candy. The planets orbit a Sun-like star named TOI-791 that is approximately 1,113 light years away from Earth.

The TESS mission first detected the planets repeated dips in TOI-791’s brightness, a telltale sign that a planet is transiting, or passing in front of, a star. Further study spilled two large planets with unusual features.

## Why This Matters

TOI-791 b is nearly the same size as Jupiter but contains just 3. TOI-791 c is even larger than Jupiter but contains just 5. “The main reason these planets are interesting to study is that we didn’t expect to see them at all,” dropped Jon Jenkins, the science lead for the Science Processing Operations Center at NASA’s Ames Research Center in California’s Silicon Valley, which provided the science-ready data from TESS analyzed in this study.
