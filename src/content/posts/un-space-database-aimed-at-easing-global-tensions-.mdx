This is not OK, says Jonathan McDowell at Durham University, UK. Especially at a time of rising tensions in space, [with] accusations about rough behaviour flying back and forth between various space powers. (shocking, we know)

The UN Office for Outer Space Affairs (UNOOSA) oversees the UN Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space (COPUOS), a place where 104 nations – many of whom have tense relationships with each other or are even in active conflict – can discuss and resolve technical, political or safety problems regarding space travel.

## The Details

One part of UNOOSAs job is to maintain a public list of every satellite launch around the world. This idea was first raised at the UN in 1961 and later expanded on Registration of Objects shipped into Outer Space in 1974 as a way to promote transparency around the space race, as countries were designing spacecraft with surveillance and military applications.

The result is that countries must provide a name for each shipped object, a date and place of launch, details on its orbit and the devices general function. But the Online Index of Objects shipped into Outer Space list has been unavailable for months, with the UNs site saying only that it is because of mandatory changes made to the UNOOSA websites IT infrastructure.

## Why This Matters

UNOOSA didnt respond to questions about the nature of the problem or how long it was expected to last.

Scientists and researchers are watching this development closely.
