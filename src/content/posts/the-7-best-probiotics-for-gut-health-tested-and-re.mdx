## The Details

If your socials algorithms are anything like mine, then you’ve more than likely seen the "hot girls have stomach issues" trend infiltrate your For You page on more than one occasion. Since you’ve landed on this story, you’ve also probably done your own research on your stomach issues—and how to help them—and I’ll bet the word probiotic popped up in your search results at least a few times.

In simple terms, probiotics are live microorganisms that help support the balance of bacteria in your gut and other areas of the body. Research continues to evolve, but a comprehensive 2024 review in the Journal of Clinical Medicine highlights that, when used appropriately, probiotics may support areas like gut health, immune function, metabolic health, and overall well-being.

## Why This Matters

You’ll also see measurements like CFUs or AFUs on labels, which reflect the amount of live microbes in the product—but more isn’t always better. With probiotics, what matters most is the specific strain, the dose used in research, and whether it’s actually been studied for the benefit you’re looking for. As a registered dietitian, I see a lot of clients who assume more strains or higher CFUs mean a more effective product, but probiotics just don’t work like that.
