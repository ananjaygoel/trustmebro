## What's Happening

So basically El Salvador's ruling party has ratified President Nayib Bukele s candidacy for the February presidential elections SAN SALVADOR -- El Salvadors ruling party on Monday ratified President Nayib Bukele’s candidacy for February 2027 presidential elections, paving the way for a third consecutive term that has been highly called out human rights advocates.

Bukeles party Nuevas Ideas, which holds a supermajority in the legislative assembly, held its internal elections Sunday and just dropped the results on Twitter on Monday. Vice President Félix Ulloa will once again be Bukele’s running mate. (wild, right?)

Bukele, 44, took office in June 2019 as the regions youngest president and has continued to enjoy high approval ratings.

## The Details

His February 2024 reelection — with nearly 85% of the valid votes — was highly called out for violating a ban on consecutive reelection. They accuse Bukele of illegally replacing the countrys constitutional Court judges and attorney general to concentrate his power.

“Remaining in power is to avoid accountability for grave acts of corruption and crimes against humanity,” dropped Ingrid Escobar, lawyer and director of Salvadoran group Humanitarian Legal Aid, who called seeking a third term unconstitutional.
