## Key Takeaways

- Helium-3 has applications in quantum computing and nuclear fusion.
- But, the main source of it today is tightly controlled – it comes from nuclear weapons.
- Specifically, from the decay of tritium, a form of hydrogen, inside those weapons.

## The Bottom Line

Helium-3 is an isotope of helium, defined of neutrons in the atoms nucleus. Helium-4, with one additional neutron, is the comparatively cheap version – a gas that fills childrens party balloons.
