## Solar Flares: The Ultimate Airplane Fryer?

Lowkey, you gotta be worried if your plane is gonna be fried by a solar flare.  And, highkey, Airbus was too worried to ignore the risk.

The European plane-maker recalled thousands of aircraft over the weekend, citing concerns that solar flares could've corrupted their navigation systems.

## What's a Solar Flare, Anyway?

A solar flare is basically a huge burst of energy from the sun that can mess with our Earth's magnetic field.  And, let's be real, it's giving us some major main character energy to think that we're basically at the sun's mercy.

#

## The Science-y Stuff

Solar flares can emit X-rays and ultraviolet radiation that can damage satellite electronics, including those used in airplane navigation systems.  And, no cap, that's a recipe for disaster.

#

## Airbus' Move

Airbus recalled over 25,000 planes, including A320 and A350 models, to check if the solar flares could've caused any issues.  It's a big move, and we're lowkey impressed that they're taking this seriously.

#

## Stats and Facts

* Over 25,000 planes were recalled
* Solar flares can cause X-ray and UV radiation * This is the first time Airbus has recalled planes due to solar flare concerns

## What's Next?

So, what's the takeaway from all this?  Well, it's giving us some serious 'plane' anxiety, but also making us understand the importance of staying vigilant when it comes to our tech.

#

## Airbus' Response

Airbus said it's working with suppliers to implement software fixes to prevent future solar flare-related issues.  And, honestly, we're just glad they're on top of it.
