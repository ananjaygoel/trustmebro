## The Details

Science news, solid photos, sky alerts. Todays top story: The sun just fired its strongest flare in days!

8 (moderate) flare erupted from sunspot region AR4436 in the northeast around 13 UTC yesterday. The blast sent a fast chunk of sun-stuff a coronal mass ejection (CME) racing into space.

## Why This Matters

Scientists have detected a fast, powerful pressure wave racing ahead of this CME. The bulk of the ejecta is heading away from Earth, but our planet could receive a glancing blow early on May 13. If so, this could trigger G1 (minor) auroras.

Scientists and researchers are watching this development closely.
