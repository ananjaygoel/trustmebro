## The Details

At the hospital, doctors noted that Jeremy had a suspected meat allergy, but ultimately blamed the episode on his asthma, which hed had since he was a toddler. He was sent home the next day.

"I made the wrong decision," Webb says, wishing she had asked for more testing. If she had, doctors might have established that Jeremy had actually been suffering from anaphylaxis, and sent him home with an EpiPen.

## Why This Matters

​​Just over a year later, in June 2022, Jeremy died after eating a beef sausage on a camping trip with friends at a beach north of Sydney. The cause of death was initially recorded simply as asthma. But in February, after an inquest pushed for , a coroner ruled that the asthma attack and resulting fatal anaphylaxis was triggered by tick-induced mammalian meat allergy (MMA).

This is the kind of health news that affects everyday decisions.

## Key Takeaways

- His death is the first in the world known to have been caused by MMA, also known as alpha-gal syndrome.
- Diagnosed cases of MMA have steadily escalated since 2007, when an Australian doctor first found out the allergy was linked to tick bites.

## The Bottom Line

Diagnosed cases of MMA have steadily escalated since 2007, when an Australian doctor first found out the allergy was linked to tick bites. Now, scientists and campaigners are warning that the condition, though easily prevented, can be unexpectedly triggered - and could pose a deadly threat.
