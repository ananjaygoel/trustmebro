The recall, filed with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) on June 17, appears to affect Waymos entire US fleet, covering 3,871 vehicles running Waymos 5th Generation automated driving system (ADS).

## The Details

NHTSA estimates 100 precent of the affected units carry the defect, which is outlined in the filed safety recall report as “under certain circumstances, the AV may enter and drive at speed in freeway-construction zones because of inappropriately prioritizing the avoidance of other freeway hazards and/or failing to recognize the construction zone. ” Waymo kicked off offering highway rides in late 2025 , and the underlying problem appears to be a failure of priority logic.

According to the NHTSA filing, the ADS sometimes flopped to recognize construction zones, and in other cases actively chose to drive through them because it was busy avoiding other hazards on the freeway. Both conditions can produce the same outcome: a driverless car at highway speed moving through a closed work zone.

## Why This Matters

The events that triggered the recall apparently began earlier this year. On April 11 and 19, Waymo vehicles in Phoenix drove past ramp closure signs into preplanned construction zones. Waymos Field Safety Committee responded operations.
