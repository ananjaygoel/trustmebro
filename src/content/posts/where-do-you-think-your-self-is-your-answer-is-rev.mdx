## The Details

Place your finger on part of your body that best represents the location of the “self”. Don’t overthink it; there is no right or wrong answer.

Simply tune into the feeling of where the essence of “you”, the core of your being, resides. Why we avoid effort even though it can improve our well-being If you are like most people, you will point to either your head or your heart.

## Why This Matters

As absurd as it may seem, a host of research suggests that your answer reflects your thinking style – whether you are being driven analysis, or intuition and emotion. And learning to shift between the two modes of being – and to do so at will – may have some surprising benefits for your decision-making. The idea that we may be led or our hearts is, of course, highly prevalent in popular culture : the metaphor has become something of a cliché.

## The Bottom Line

Through self-report questionnaires, they showed that so-called “head-locators” were more likely to describe themselves as rational and logical, whereas “heart-locators” saw themselves as more emotionally driven. And those perceptions seemed to be reflected in more objective measures of behaviour.
