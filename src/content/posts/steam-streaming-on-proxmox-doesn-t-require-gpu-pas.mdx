It's a pain: block the driver, wrangle IOMMU groups, dedicate the entire GPU to one VM, and pray that the next kernel update doesn't break everything. My homelab already runs half a dozen services on Proxmox , and giving up the only GPU in the machine to a single VM just feels wasteful.

## Why This Matters

The thing is, you don't need passthrough at all . An LXC container s the host kernel, so it can also the GPU. The host keeps it, the container borrows it when needed, and nothing needs blocking.

This is part of the broader shift happening across the tech industry right now.
