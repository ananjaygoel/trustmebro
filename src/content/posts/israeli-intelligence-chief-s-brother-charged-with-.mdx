Zini, 50, a reservist on active duty in the Israeli military, is dropped to have smuggled 14 cartons of cigarettes - 7,000 individual packets - in exchange for a total of 365,000 shekels ($117,000; £86,000).

## The Details

His brother, the just appointed Shin Bet chief David Zini, is not suspected of any wrongdoing. Israel has tightly controlled the flow of humanitarian aid and other goods into Gaza during the conflict with Hamas.

The charges are part of a wider set of indictments against more than a dozen individuals, alleging that an organised crime ring smuggled brand new, top-end iPhones, car parts, and other electronics into Gaza. The sale of smuggled tobacco and cigarettes alone put "hundreds of millions of shekels into Hamass coffers since the start of the war", the indictment alleges.

## Why This Matters

Prosecutors say the smuggling operation began in the summer of 2025, before the ceasefire in October, when the fighting was still raging in the strip. They say the smugglers pretended they were entering Gaza as part of their military duties, misleading Israeli soldiers on the border crossing points. The indictment against Bezalel Zini also accuses him of fraud, receiving a bribe, and tax offences.
