## The Details

Production accuracy never moved. Pilots kept complaining about the same wrong answers.

Six months in, the bug was in the parser. RAG is not ML, and the ML toolkit solves the wrong problem.

## Why This Matters

This is the single most expensive misconception in enterprise RAG today. It costs months of careful work, the wrong people on the wrong tasks, and a quiet erosion of trust in the system. RAG looks enough like ML that the ML toolkit feels like the natural next step.

This adds to the ongoing AI race that's captivating the tech world.

## Key Takeaways

- The instincts (hyperparameter optimization, evaluation datasets, explainability frameworks) are not wrong in isolation.
- They are imported from the wrong field.
- The methods that work for training models don’t work for assembling search systems.
- The point is not that ML is rough.

## The Bottom Line

You don’t know the answer in advance. The model learns from labeled examples, generalizes to new inputs, and produces a prediction.
