## The Details

I’m Kiril Kazlou, a data engineer at Mindbox. Our team regularly recalculates business metrics for clients — which means we’re constantly building data marts for billing and analytics, pulling from dozens of different sources.

For a long time, we relied on PySpark for all our data processing. You can’t fr work with PySpark without Python experience.

## Why This Matters

Every new pipeline required a developer. And that meant waiting — sometimes for weeks. In this post, I’ll walk you through how we built an internal data platform where an analyst or product manager can spin up a regularly updated pipeline four YAML files.

The AI space continues to evolve at a wild pace, with developments like this becoming more common.

## Key Takeaways

- Why PySpark Was Slowing Us Down Let me illustrate the pain with a textbook example — calculating MAU (Monthly Active Users).
- On the surface, this feels like a simple SQL job: COUNT(DISTINCT customerId) across a few tables over a time window.
- A full week just to ship a MAU counter.

## The Bottom Line

The developer clarified details, wrote PySpark code, went through code review, configured the DAG, and deployed. What we actually wanted was for analysts and product managers — the people who understand the business logic best and are fluent in SQL and YAML — to handle this themselves.
