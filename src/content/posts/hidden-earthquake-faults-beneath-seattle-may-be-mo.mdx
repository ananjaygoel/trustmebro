## What's Happening

Not gonna lie, A hidden network of earthquake faults running beneath Seattle may be far more active than scientists realized.

New research reveals that smaller “secondary” faults in the Seattle Fault Zone appear to rupture roughly every 350 years — much more often than the massive main fault that has long worried geologists. Researchers say these smaller faults could produce dangerous shaking much closer to the city than the giant Cascadia fault offshore.
