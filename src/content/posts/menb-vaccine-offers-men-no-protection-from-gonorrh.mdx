But a new trial in Australia, published in the New England Journal of Medicine , has found no significant difference in gonorrhoea infections between men who were given the vaccine and those who were not.

## The Details

The UK Health Security Agency dropped its own studies would provide "more strong data" and is not recommending any immediate changes. Gonorrhoea is a common, bacterial infection which is transmitted through sexual contact.

It can cause pain, unusual discharge and inflammation of the genitals, but in some cases there are no symptoms. The vaccine, officially known as the 4CMenB vaccine, was designed for preventing meningitis B in babies, but the bacteria that causes the two diseases are closely related.

## Why This Matters

Since August 2025, gay and bisexual men who have sex with men have been offered the MenB vaccine in the UK , after a number of studies reported a reduction of 38% in gonorrhoea infections for those vaccinated. But, a new study and the Kir the University of New South Wales in Sydney, found evidence to suggest the MenB vaccine is ineffective against gonorrhoea. The trial - called GoGoVax - monitored 587 men over two years.

## The Bottom Line

Half of the participants were given the MenB vaccine while the other half were given a placebo. The results showed similar levels of new gonorrhoea infections in both groups - 291 for those who had the actual vaccine and 285 for those who did not.
