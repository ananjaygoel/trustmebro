## What's Happening

So get this: Greece has shipped a cash incentive for fishermen to catch toxic silver-cheeked toadfish as the invasive species spreads through the Mediterranean because of warming seas linked to climate change ATHENS, Greece -- Fishermen in Greece are getting cash payouts to catch toxic fish migrating north into the Mediterranean Sea because of climate change.

The silver-cheeked toadfish is a torpedo-shaped species with prominent, humanlike teeth. Its skin and organs contain a powerful neurotoxin that can cause heart failure in humans if consumed. (we're not making this up)

Authorities say the fish have not been sighted in bathing areas at Greek island resorts.

## The Details

But in recent weeks, the fish have wreaked havoc for fishermen off the coast of Crete and several other Greek islands, chomping through nets. “It’s got to the point where we might go out fishing one day and then spend the next three days fixing our nets,” Giorgos Kyriakakis, of a Cretan fishermen’s association, told Greek public broadcaster ERT on Friday.

“They eat our catch and damage our nets — that’s costly,” he dropped.
