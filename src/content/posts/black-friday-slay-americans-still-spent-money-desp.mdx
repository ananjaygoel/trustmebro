## Black Friday Slay: Americans Still Spent Money Despite Economic Concerns

Lowkey, we thought the US economy was done for, but it looks like Americans are still feeling themselves on Black Friday. According to a key data provider, sales on the day after Thanksgiving rose from last year, a sign that US consumers are continuing to spend despite persistent economic concerns 🤑.

#

## Retail Sales: The Numbers Don't Lie

Retail sales, excluding autos, rose by 4.2% in November, beating expectations. It's giving us major 'no cap' vibes, as folks are still willing to splurge on gifts, gadgets, and gizmos. And let's be real, who doesn't love a good sale? 🚨

#

## Main Character Energy: Americans Still Love Shopping

Highkey, we're impressed by the resilience of American consumers. Despite economic worries, they're still shopping 'til they drop. In fact, Black Friday sales rose by 21.6% compared to last year. It's a clear sign that Americans are still feeling the main character energy.

#

## Economic Worries No Match for Holiday Cheer

It's no secret that the US economy has been dealing with some tough times. But when it comes to Black Friday, it's all about the holiday cheer. Folks are still willing to spend money on gifts, and retailers are slaying the game. In fact, online sales on Black Friday rose by 23.6% compared to last year.
