## The Details

The Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory, better known as Swift, has been orbiting Earth since 2004. Swift has been studying the most powerful explosions in the universe: gamma-ray bursts.

Now, its orbit is now rapidly decaying. Swift is falling back to Earth, and if nothing stops it, it will burn up in our atmosphere this fall in a spectacular, fiery show.

## Why This Matters

But Swift is still doing solid science, so NASA wants to extend the life of the mission. To do so, it will have to boost the spacecraft back into a stable orbit. In late 2025, Katalyst Space clutched a contract with NASA to push Swift back into nearly its original orbit.
