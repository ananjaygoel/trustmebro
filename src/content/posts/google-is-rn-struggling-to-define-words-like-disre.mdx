## What's Happening

Not gonna lie, The search engine's definitions have been replaced with AI Overviews.

News AI Google is rn struggling to define words like disregard, stop and ignore The search engine's definitions have been replaced with AI Overviews. By Ian Carlos Campbell Updated: 5:47 pm EST Karissa Bell for Engadget Google appears to be running into some hiccups after the company began rolling out its updated, and even more AI-focused search experience at I/O 2026. (it feels like chaos)

Rn, searching for the words "disregard," "stop" or "ignore" on Google no longer displays a snippet with a definition, and instead offers an AI Overview and a lot of blank space.

## The Details

Because users have complained about the issue on socials, and publications like TechCrunch and Macrumors have reported on it, even if you don't get a definition, you might still get a collection of links to articles documenting the issue before the traditional list of links. Multiple members of Engadget's staff were able to recreate the strange AI Overview responses with their own personal Google searches.

In Incognito Mode, Google responded correctly once usual snippet with the definition, and flopped a second time responding with an AI Overview. Links to online dictionaries still appear under these incorrect results, but you have to scroll past an AI Overview or a grid of articles to actually get to them.

## Why This Matters

Ian Carlos Campbell for Engadget Engadget contacted Google for more information. The company responded acknowledging the issue and dropped a fix was incoming: "We're aware that AI Overviews are misinterpreting some action-related queries, and we're working on a fix, which will roll out soon," a spokesperson dropped. In the grand scheme of things, Google not automatically displaying a definition isn't as rough as recommending people put glue on pizza, one of the issues the company dealt with when it first shipped AI Overviews.
