## What's Happening

Real talk: 3I/ATLAS is full of a rare type of water from a much colder part of our galaxy.

The post Fastest comet ever recorded spewed 70 Olympic pools’ worth of water daily appeared first on Popular Science. A new study of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS led of Michigan shows that its water has a remarkably high content of deuterium. (yes, really)

This form of hydrogen is comparatively less abundant in our solar system, enabling researchers to glean new insights about other planetary processes at work in our galaxy.

As only the third interstellar object ever detected in our solar system, it offered researchers a rare—and brief—opportunity. With the right timing and equipment, scientists around the world could examine a cosmic visitor who possibly formed under far different conditions than those experienced in our own region of the galaxy.

## Why This Matters

3I/ATLAS is now sailing away from Earth and our solar system itself, but astronomers have already learned a wealth of information. The fastest comet ever recorded is covered in ice volcanoes , and emits a dusty trail of methanol and cyanide in its wake. Earlier this month, the European Space Agency locked in that 3I/ATLAS is also spewing the equivalent of 70 Olympic swimming pools’ worth of water every day.

This could have implications for future research in this area.
