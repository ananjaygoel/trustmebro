## What's Happening A new story trailer for Dawn of War 4 has just been revealed, sending ripples through the Warhammer community. The big news? The Dark Angels are officially making their debut as a playable sub-faction. But that's not all. The trailer also teases the long-awaited return of Lion El' Johnson, the "10,000+ year-old grandpa" Primarch, who appears to finally be interacting with his legion. ## Why This Matters The inclusion of the Dark Angels as a playable sub-faction is a significant win for fans of this secretive Space Marine chapter. Their unique lore and potential tactical depth could offer fresh gameplay experiences. However, the real showstopper is Lion El' Johnson, the legendary Primarch of the First Legion. His awakening after millennia isn't just fan service; it signals a monumental shift for the game's narrative and potentially the wider Warhammer 40,000 universe. - Lion's return could profoundly impact the Dark Angels' internal conflicts, especially their relentless hunt for the Fallen.
- It opens the door for other Primarchs to make appearances, potentially escalating the scale of future conflicts in Dawn of War 4.
- His presence promises a deeper, more character-driven story, moving beyond generic faction warfare to explore ancient grudges and loyalties. ## The Bottom Line This Dawn of War 4 trailer is a colossal reveal, promising a richer story, beloved characters, and exciting new strategic options. It's clear the developers are aiming for something truly epic with this installment. What other legendary figures might Dawn of War 4 bring back from the depths of Warhammer lore?
