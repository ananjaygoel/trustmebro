## The Details

Bats pollinate plants , including many important food crops, when they stop drink nectar . Their guano is mined from caves for fertilizer .

And they eat a lot of bugs – the kinds that bother people ( think mosquitoes ) and others that destroy crops that humans depend on for food. Sadly, bat populations are declining rapidly in North America .

## Why This Matters

A driving force is a fungal disease known as white-nose syndrome , which has spread among bats throughout the United States. When a bat population crashes, fewer bats are around to eat bothersome insects. All those additional insects can do serious damage.

The business implications here could be significant in the coming months.
