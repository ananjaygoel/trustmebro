## The Details

P]:text-cms-story-body-color-text clearfix max-w-170 mt-7. 5 mb-10 mx-auto" data-dateline data-r-content GUADALAJARA, Jalisco — Before the World Cup, haters and the fandom of the Mexican national team debated whether Javier Aguirre’s third stint as head coach was the right decision.

Two games later, the coach has led Mexico to the tournament’s knockout round. Thursday’s 1-0 victory over South Korea at Guadalajara Stadium made the World Cup co-host the first in the tournament to advance while also clinching the top spot in Group A with one group play game remaining.

## Why This Matters

After South Korea’s goalkeeper fumbled a ball, Luis Romo scored the goal that gave the Mexicans six points and pushed them to the top of the standings. El Tri will close out the first round against Czechia (6 p. ) at Azteca Stadium on Wednesday.
