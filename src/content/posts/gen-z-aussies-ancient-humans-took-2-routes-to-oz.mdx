## Ancient Humans Ate the Competition to Reach Australia

Scientists have been trying to figure out how our ancestors made it to the land of Oz for ages. Like, we're talking 60,000 years ago. Now, a genetic study has finally spilled the tea on the two routes they took to get there. 🤯  #

## Route 1: The Coastal Crew  The first route was taken by coastal dwellers who followed the shoreline. This was no easy feat, no cap. They had to navigate through treacherous waters, deal with harsh weather conditions, and avoid getting caught in the undertow. Sounds like a real-life game of 'Among Us' 🚣‍♀️. But, lowkey, it's giving us major respect for our ancestors.  #

## Route 2: The Inland Invasion  The second route was taken by inland travelers who trekked across the continent. This was like, a whole different level of struggle. They had to contend with harsh desert conditions, limited resources, and the occasional dust storm. Yikes. It's like they had to survive a 'TikTok challenge' 🌵, but, you know, for real life.

## The Science Behind the Study  The genetic study analyzed DNA samples from modern-day Australians and found that they can be divided into two groups: the coastal and inland populations. This suggests that our ancestors took two distinct routes to reach the land down under. The study also revealed that the coastal group was more genetically diverse, which is, like, a big deal. It means that they had a more complex social structure and were more adaptable to their environment.

## The Takeaway  So, there you have it. Our ancestors took two routes to reach Australia, and it's giving us major main character energy. We're talking about people who were, like, 100% that they could make it to the other side of the world. And, honestly, it's pretty inspiring. Next time you're feeling stuck, just remember: our ancestors had to navigate treacherous waters and harsh desert conditions to get to their destination. That's, like, the ultimate lowkey highkey motivation. 💪
