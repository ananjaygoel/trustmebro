## What's Happening

Not gonna lie, Another heatwave is on its way and set to last majorly longer as Stav Danaos explains.

New heatwave likely to peak at 34C with week-long health alert issued Image source, PA By Stav Danaos Lead Weather Presenter Published 4 July 2026 A new heatwave is starting to build across parts of the UK from this weekend, with forecasters warning that temperatures could reach 33C or 34C. For some areas this will mark the third heatwave of the year. (we're not making this up)

It comes as week-long yellow heat health alerts have been issued Health Security Agency from the Midlands and East Anglia down across both the south-east and south-west of England.

## The Details

The highest temperatures are expected in southern England and while maybe not as intense as the record-breaking heatwave in June , it may last a lot longer. Image caption, Temperatures are expected to climb across western Europe in the week ahead, with many cities exceeding 30C again How long will this heatwave last?

Temperatures in the south of the UK will continue to rise gradually over the weekend as high pressure builds from the Azores. It will turn warmer still from Monday with the heat expected to peak during the second half of the week across England and Wales.

## Why This Matters

Some areas are likely to meet official heatwave criteria . The highest temperatures will be focused across southern and eastern England, where values could widely exceed 30C. In the north-east of England and eastern Scotland the mid to high-20s are possible.

Scientists and researchers are watching this development closely.
