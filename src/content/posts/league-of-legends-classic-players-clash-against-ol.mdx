## The Details

This new (or old) spin on League of Legends is launching for the whole world on the 29th July, though early games are being played on the game's PBE (or: public beta environment). In it, we can see the old Summoner's Rift map in all its glory, removed abilities and items running rampant, and champions with their modern skins.

## Key Takeaways

- This includes a Classic skin token, though they appear scarcely.

- Some freebies are nice, and should cover the main champions a player would use in League Classic.
- But, this does leave the vast majority of the champions present in League of Legends Classic stuck with their modern models.

## The Bottom Line

With 60 champions present at launch, those who would want to play the remaining 56 champions with their classic look will have to buy a token from the store. This costs 500 Riot Points, or roughly $5.
