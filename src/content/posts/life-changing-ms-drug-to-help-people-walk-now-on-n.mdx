## The Details

The drug is already available elsewhere in the UK. Multiple sclerosis is caused system attacking the protective covering around nerve cells - known as myelin.

This damage impairs the ability of nerves to carry electrical messages from the brain to the rest of the body - including those needed for the precise control of muscles needed to walk. Fampridine is known as a "signal booster" as it helps those electrical messages still get through.

## Why This Matters

Clinical trials showed around four in 10 people taking the drug had improvements in their walking speed and in how long they could walk for. This could be the difference between getting around the house or needing help at home or enabling people to go out and see friends and family. Current NHS support includes physiotherapy and walking aids.

Medical professionals are taking note of this development.
