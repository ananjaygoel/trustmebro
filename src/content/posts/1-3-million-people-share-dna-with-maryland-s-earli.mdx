## The Details

In 1634, English settlers established St. Mary’s City as the first permanent outpost in the colony of Maryland.

Many of these early residents were ultimately buried in the town’s Chapel Field cemetery, including 49 colonists between the town’s founding and 1734. Just, geneticists collaborating between Harvard University, the Smithsonian Institute, and genetics company 23AndMe analyzed these before unidentified remains as part of a larger genealogical project tracing colonial migration across the United States.
