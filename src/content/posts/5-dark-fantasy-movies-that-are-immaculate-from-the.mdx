While the mere utterance of the word fantasy will, for most people, conjure images of magical kingdoms, dragons and the like, the genre can encompass worlds and characters far more vast and varied.

## The Details

A fantasy is, by definition, something divorced from reality, fiction grounded in something outside the scientific world. When it comes to movies, it can mean many things: we've got high fantasy, low fantasy, magical realism, historical fantasy, science fantasy, epic fantasy, and urban fantasy.

Those are but a few permutations that can, and do, intersect and overlap with regular frequency. There's no one thing that makes something fantasy, but you know fantasy when you see it, even when it's dark.

## Why This Matters

Dark fantasy has been a part of the genre for as long as we've been telling stories. The epic poem Beowulf could be considered dark fantasy, and most folklore and fairytales have much darker, or more violent, content within them than their Disneyfied counterparts would suggest. Darkness has always been a part of fantasy storytelling, and some movies have chosen to embrace it.

The entertainment world moves fast, and this is a prime example.

## The Bottom Line

There are many good, and even solid, fantasy movies . From childhood nightmare inducers like Return to Oz or modern fantasy horror films like Tigers Are Not Afraid , there's no shortage of films that are equal parts fantastical and frightsome.
