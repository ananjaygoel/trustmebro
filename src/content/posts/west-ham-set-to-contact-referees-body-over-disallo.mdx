## What's Happening

Alright so West Ham will contact referees' body the Professional Game Match Officials (PGMO) over their disallowed equaliser against Arsenal on Sunday.

West Ham set to contact referees body over disallowed goal Figure caption, West Ham have injury-time goal disallowed as Arsenal go five clear By Sami Mokbel Senior football correspondent Published 11 May 2026, 06:08 BST Updated 24 minutes ago West Ham are set to contact referees body the Professional Game Match Officials (PGMO) over their late equaliser being disallowed against Arsenal . The Hammers had appeared to have dealt a significant blow to the Gunners title hopes - as well as boosting their own Premier League survival prospects - when Callum Wilson struck in stoppage time on Sunday. (yes, really)

But after a video assistant referee (VAR) review, the goal was ruled out for a foul Arsenal goalkeeper David Raya.

## The Details

West Ham will raise concerns and request further explanation from PGMO following the controversial decision. BBC Sport understands they will also request the audio between referee Chris Kavanagh and VAR Darren England.

The Gunners went on to win 1-0, taking them five points clear of Manchester City at the top of the Premier League and making West Ham s future that bit more uncertain - as they sit in 18th place, with Tottenham Hotspur a point above them before the host Leeds on Monday. Former England and Manchester United striker Wayne Rooney believes VAR "did a fr good job" in disallowing the goal.
