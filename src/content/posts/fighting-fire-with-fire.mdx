NASA Earth Observatory/Michala Garrison In May and June of most years, NASA satellites typically begin to detect large numbers of wildland fires throughout the Top End and Arnhem Land regions of Australia’s Northern Territory.

## The Details

On some days, especially in the afternoon, the blazes can resemble sizable wildfires in satellite imagery, spreading widely and producing expansive smoke plumes. That was the case when NASA's Aqua satellite acquired this image of smoke and fires on the afternoon of .

Often, but, fires burning in this area look smaller and less imposing. In the mornings just a few days earlier and later , for instance, NASA satellites detected little smoke despite observing many thermal anomalies , or hotspots, that indicated fire activity.

## Why This Matters

The pattern of burning, location, and timing are consistent with prescribed fires lit intentionally to manage the landscape. Land managers tend to light fires in the morning, and smoke builds over the course of the day.

This could have implications for future research in this area.
